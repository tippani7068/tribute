About Mahatma Gandhi

Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi, commonly known as Mahatma Gandhi, was a leader of the Indian independence movement against British colonial rule. Born on October 2, 1869, in Porbandar, India, he became known for his nonviolent civil disobedience tactics.

Gandhi's philosophy of nonviolence (Ahimsa) and his commitment to truth (Satyagraha) inspired millions and played a crucial role in India's struggle for freedom. His methods of peaceful resistance and civil disobedience were instrumental in achieving India's independence in 1947.

One of his famous quotes is: "You must be the change you want to see in the world."

Throughout his life, Gandhi advocated for social justice, equality, and harmony among different communities. He was a symbol of hope and inspiration, not just for India but for people all around the world.
